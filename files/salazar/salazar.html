Franciscus Cervantes de Salazar (1514-1575) was a Spaniard scholar and friend of Iohannes Vives noted for his works in both Latin and Spanish. From 1550 to his death, he was a leading academic figure in Mexico, serving as rector of the Pontifical University twice. This book has an interesting commentary on three of the colloquia: Icazbalceta, 1875. These colloquia were first extracted from a longer work, found here: University of Texas. See also Laird, 2024 and Wikipedia.
